# OpenStrengths Monetization Strategy

**Version:** 1.0
**Date:** November 14, 2025
**Status:** Strategic Planning (Not yet integrated into whitepaper)

---

## Executive Summary

OpenStrengths will operate on a freemium model with two primary revenue streams:

1. **Individual Passport:** $40/year subscription focused on professional credibility, longitudinal tracking, and AI-powered life event attribution
2. **Organization Platform:** $8-25/seat/month tiered pricing for team collaboration, talent optimization, and workforce analytics

**Core Philosophy:** Free assessments maximize adoption and create network effects. Premium tiers provide professional-grade tools and insights that justify clear ROI.

---

## Part 1: Individual Monetization

### Free Tier - "Personal Use"

**Target:** Individuals exploring strengths for personal development

**Features:**
- Take core assessment (one time)
- See current results
- Export basic PDF
- Share generic results link
- **Limitation:** Results expire after 1 year (must retake to refresh)
- **Limitation:** No historical tracking
- **Limitation:** No professional credentialing

**Value Proposition:** "Understand your strengths for free"

**Strategic Purpose:**
- Maximize adoption
- Create word-of-mouth growth
- Build user base for organizational sales pipeline
- Generate data for platform improvement

**OpenStrengths Advantages:**
- Free individual assessments (lower adoption barrier)
- Modern, employee + manager facing UX
- AI-powered insights (competitors lack this)
- Integration ecosystem (API-first)
- 10x cheaper than CliftonStrengths at every tier
- Multiple entry points (not just one-time assessment)

### Product Vision: "Team Strengths OS"

**Not:** A one-time assessment tool managers use
**Instead:** An ongoing platform where both employees and managers actively engage weekly

**Core Insight:**
Strengths are most valuable when integrated into ongoing work - hiring, team building, 1:1s, performance reviews, project staffing, career development.

## Open Questions & Risks

### Individual Tier

**Questions:**
1. Is 3% conversion realistic? (Industry standard for freemium SaaS: 2-5%)
2. Will users actually log life events consistently?
3. How much will LLM attribution cost at scale?
4. What's the annual churn rate? (Assuming 25%, may be lower)

**Risks:**
1. Life event tracking feels like too much work ‚Üí low engagement
2. LLM insights aren't compelling enough to justify $40/year
3. Free tier is "good enough" for most users ‚Üí low conversion
4. Annual billing makes it hard to test pricing iterations

**Mitigation:**
- Make life event logging dead simple (1-click common events)
- Test pricing with A/B: $40/year vs $5/month
- Add more passive value (integrations that work without logging in)
- Monitor engagement metrics closely in first 6 months

---

### Organization Tier

**Questions:**
1. Is $8/seat too expensive for startups?
2. What's the minimum viable feature set for Starter tier?
3. How long is sales cycle for Professional/Enterprise?
4. What integrations are table-stakes vs nice-to-have?

**Risks:**
1. Free trial ‚Üí paid conversion is low (<20%)
2. Organizations view this as "nice to have" not "must have"
3. Competitors (CliftonStrengths) respond aggressively
4. Feature bloat trying to satisfy enterprise needs
5. Customer success costs eat into margins at low price points

**Mitigation:**
- Offer "free forever for first 5 users" to reduce barrier
- Focus on clear ROI metrics (reduced turnover, better hiring)
- Start with SMB/mid-market (faster sales cycles, lower CAC)
- Keep Starter tier feature set minimal and focused
- Build self-serve onboarding (reduce CS burden)
